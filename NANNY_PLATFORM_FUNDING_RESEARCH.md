## 1. ISRAEL INNOVATION AUTHORITY (IIA) GRANTS 2025-2026

### 1.1 Ideation Program (Tnufa)

The Ideation (Tnufa) Incentive Program is the IIA's entry-level grant for fledgling entrepreneurs. It does **not** require a registered company -- entrepreneurs can apply as individuals.

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Grant Amount** | Up to NIS 200,000 (80% of approved NIS 250,000 budget) |
| **Structure** | Single application (previously two NIS 100K cycles) |
| **Equity Taken** | **None** -- fully non-dilutive |
| **Repayment** | Royalties from future product sales (typically 3-5% of revenue until grant repaid) |
| **Duration** | Up to 2 years |
| **Eligible Costs** | Prototyping, IP protection, business development, materials, subcontractors, consultants, patent attorneys, exhibitions. **Not for salaries or overhead.** |
| **Application Process** | Streamlined (recently simplified). Decision within ~4-9 weeks. |
| **Annual Grants** | ~100 entrepreneurs per year |
| **Applicant** | Individual entrepreneur or early-stage company |

**Does a childcare marketplace qualify?** The IIA evaluates projects on five criteria: (1) technical novelty; (2) economic viability and revenue generation for Israel; (3) team capability; (4) collaboration with partners; (5) application clarity. A marketplace platform with matching algorithms, verification systems, and AI-driven recommendations could qualify if positioned as a **technology platform** rather than a simple directory. The social impact angle (women's workforce participation, child safety) strengthens the application but is not a primary criterion -- the IIA primarily funds **technological innovation**.

**Strategic assessment:** Tnufa is excellent for initial validation and prototype funding, but NIS 200K covers only ~1.4 months of the projected NIS 143K/mo pre-launch burn. Its real value is the **signal** -- IIA approval serves as third-party validation for follow-on investors.

### 1.2 Startup Fund -- Pre-Seed

This is the IIA's most relevant program for the nanny platform at the current stage.

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Grant Amount** | Up to NIS 1.5M (60% of the pre-seed investment round) |
| **Equity Taken** | **None** -- fully non-dilutive |
| **Repayment** | Royalties from future product sales |
| **How It Works** | IIA matches 60% of a private pre-seed round. If founders raise NIS 2.5M from private investors, IIA adds NIS 1.5M. |
| **Requirement** | Must have a private investor committed (the IIA co-invests alongside private capital) |
| **Application Process** | Robust due diligence by IIA technology experts. ~9 weeks for startup division decisions (90% of applicants). |
| **Post-Approval** | Grant agreement signed within 10 calendar days; first disbursement follows immediately. |

**Female Founder Bonus:** Companies where at least one main entrepreneur is from an underrepresented population in high-tech (women, Arabs, Ultra-Orthodox) or based in Israel's periphery receive an **additional 10% on the grant**. This means:
- Standard pre-seed grant: up to NIS 1.5M (60% of round)
- **Female founder pre-seed grant: up to NIS 1.65M (66% of round)**

This is a significant advantage. A female-founded nanny platform targeting families could receive NIS 1.65M in non-dilutive capital.

### 1.3 Startup Fund -- Seed

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Grant Amount** | Up to NIS 5M (50% of the seed investment round) |
| **Equity Taken** | **None** -- non-dilutive |
| **Repayment** | Royalties from product sales |
| **How It Works** | IIA matches 50% of a private seed round. If founders raise NIS 10M from VCs, IIA adds NIS 5M. |
| **Female Founder Bonus** | Up to NIS 5.5M (55% of round) |

### 1.4 Startup Fund -- Series A

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Grant Amount** | Up to NIS 15M (30% of Series A round) |
| **Equity Taken** | **None** |
| **Female Founder Bonus** | Up to NIS 16.5M (33% of round) |

### 1.5 IIA Requirements & Obligations

All IIA grant recipients must comply with these conditions:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Israeli Company** | Must be a registered Israeli company (Ltd. / Hevra Be'eravon Mugbelet) |
| **R&D in Israel** | Core R&D activities must be performed in Israel |
| **IP Stays in Israel** | IP developed with IIA funding cannot be transferred abroad without IIA consent |
| **Production in Israel** | Manufacturing/operations must be in Israel for 5 years (unless special approval) |
| **Royalty Payments** | 3-5% of product revenue until 100% of grant + interest is repaid |
| **Reporting** | Periodic progress reports, often requiring CPA certification |
| **Designated Use** | Funds must be used only for approved budget items |
| **No Transfer** | Cannot sell, transfer, or exclusively license IIA-funded IP without prior IIA written consent |

**Key implication for a marketplace:** The IP restriction means the platform's core technology (matching algorithms, verification systems, etc.) must remain in Israel. This is not a constraint for an Israel-focused platform. If expanding internationally later, the company would need IIA approval to operate technology abroad (routinely granted for SaaS/marketplace businesses serving foreign markets, as the IP stays in Israel).

### 1.6 IIA Strategic Summary

## 2. ISRAELI PRE-SEED / SEED VC LANDSCAPE 2025-2026

### 2.1 Market Context

| Metric | 2025-2026 Data |
|--------|----------------|
| **Total Israeli startup funding (2025)** | $15.6B (major recovery driven by AI) |
| **Pre-seed + seed funding (H1 2025)** | $607M, up 50% vs H2 2024 |
| **Average seed round size** | $4.2M (up from $3.1M in 2023) |
| **Median Israeli pre-seed round** | $825K ($750K VC + $75K angel) |
| **Avg pre-seed founder dilution** | ~12% |
| **Avg seed founder dilution** | 15-25% |
| **Avg time Seed to Series A** | 35 months (~3 years) |
| **Dominant sectors (70% of capital)** | Cybersecurity + Generative AI |
| **Government VC injection (2024-2026)** | ~$1B/year into Israeli VC funds |

**Critical insight for a consumer marketplace:** Israeli VCs have dramatically shifted toward AI and cybersecurity. Consumer marketplace deals represent a small fraction of the ecosystem. This means:
- Fewer VCs will be interested compared to a B2B SaaS or AI startup
- The nanny platform must demonstrate a **technology moat** (AI matching, verification tech) to attract VC interest
- The social impact angle may attract impact-focused investors but not mainstream Israeli VCs
- The IIA grant serves as a crucial signal to private investors

### 2.3 VC Investment Precedent in Childcare/Parenting

Israeli VCs have **very limited** direct precedent in childcare/parenting marketplaces:

- **No major Israeli VC-backed childcare marketplace** found in research
- Globally, Peanut (parenting community) raised $12M Series A from EQT Ventures (London)
- Koru Kids (nanny sharing, UK) raised GBP 10M Series A from Atomico
- Israel has baby-tech startups (monitoring, health) but not marketplace-focused childcare
- Villiger (parent support app) exists but has no major VC backing found

**Implication:** The founder will need to educate VCs on the opportunity. The pitch must emphasize: (1) total addressable market in Israel; (2) technology moat (not just a directory); (3) expansion potential; (4) comparison to successful international models (Care.com, Bubble, UrbanSitter).

**Strategy to identify relevant angels:**

1. **Parent-founders in tech:** Target successful Israeli tech founders who are also parents (especially mothers). They understand the pain point personally.
2. **iAngels women's angel program:** iAngels actively recruits women into angel investing -- many are mothers in tech who would resonate with a childcare platform.
3. **8200 Alumni Network:** Israel's largest tech alumni network. Many alumni are now parents in their 30s-40s with capital to invest.
4. **Israeli mom influencers with capital:** Some larger Israeli parenting influencers have moved into angel investing.
5. **Corporate executives in HR/benefits:** HR leaders at Israeli tech companies who deal with employee childcare challenges.

### 3.4 Notable Israeli Angels for Consumer/Marketplace

Target angels who have invested in consumer or marketplace companies:
- Founders/exits from ironSource, Monday.com, Wix, Fiverr, Lemonade

## 5. ALTERNATIVE FUNDING SOURCES

### 5.1 Revenue-Based Financing (RBF)

| Provider | Model | Israel Availability | Minimum Revenue | Notes |
|----------|-------|--------------------|-----------------:|-------|
| **Clearco** | Advance on future revenue | Limited/uncertain | ~$10K MRR | Laid off 25% staff in 2022. Focused on e-commerce/DTC, not marketplaces. |
| **Pipe** | Revenue trading marketplace | Not Israel-focused | Established revenue | Marketplace model. Not available for pre-revenue startups. |
| **Capchase** | Direct lending on SaaS revenue | May serve Israeli companies | $100K+ ARR | Uses own balance sheet. SaaS-focused, not marketplace. |

**Assessment:** RBF is **not viable** for the nanny platform at launch. These providers require established, predictable recurring revenue ($100K+ ARR minimum). RBF becomes relevant only after Month 12-18 when revenue is established. Not a pre-launch funding option.

### 5.2 Equity Crowdfunding

| Platform | Details | Regulation |
|----------|---------|------------|
| **PipelBiz** | Israeli equity crowdfunding platform launched 2015, regulated since 2018 | Securities Regulations (Offer of Securities through an Offering Coordinator), 2017 |
| **OurCrowd** | Hybrid: equity crowdfunding + managed VC fund | ISA regulated. Accredited investors only. |
| **Minimum Investment** | As low as NIS 2,500 (~$700) on PipelBiz | Broader investor access than traditional VC |

**Market size:** Equity crowdfunding in Israel raised ~ILS 60M ($17M) in 2020. Still a small market but growing.

**Assessment for nanny platform:** Equity crowdfunding could be interesting as a **community-building + fundraising hybrid**. Parents investing in the platform they use creates alignment. However:
- Raises are typically smaller (NIS 500K-2M range)
- Managing many small shareholders is administratively burdensome
- May complicate future VC rounds (messy cap table)
- Best used as a **supplement** to a lead investor, not the primary raise

### 5.3 Government Social Impact Programs

| Program | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **IIA Startup Fund** | Primary vehicle (see Section 1). Social impact is not a separate criterion but strengthens applications. |
| **Ministry of Economy** | Occasional programs for women's employment and childcare infrastructure. Not typically for tech startups. |
| **JDC (Joint) / Tevet** | Funds workforce development programs. Could be a partner rather than funder. |
| **National Insurance Institute** | Funds childcare subsidies, not startup development. |

**Assessment:** There is no dedicated Israeli government grant specifically for childcare tech platforms. The IIA Startup Fund remains the best government funding vehicle, with the female founder bonus being the most relevant social impact incentive.

### 5.4 Corporate Partnerships as Funding

| Potential Partner Type | Value | Likelihood |
|-----------------------|-------|------------|
| **Israeli tech companies (employee benefits)** | Pilot contracts + potential strategic investment | Medium-High. Companies like Monday, Wix, Check Point spend heavily on employee perks. |
| **Insurance companies** | Data partnership + sponsorship | Medium. Home insurance companies interested in verified childcare. |
| **Real estate / community management** | Distribution channel + sponsorship | Medium. Vaad Bayit / building management integration. |
| **Payment companies** | Infrastructure partnership | Low-Medium. PayPlus, Tranzila might offer favorable terms, not capital. |

**Assessment:** Corporate partnerships are valuable for **revenue** and **distribution** but rarely provide significant startup capital. Best pursued post-MVP as B2B contracts rather than as a funding strategy.

## 7. TOP RECOMMENDATION

### Recommended Strategy: **A+B Hybrid -- "IIA-Anchored Angel Round"**

The optimal strategy combines the best elements of Strategy A (grant-first) and Strategy B (angel-first) into a single, parallelized approach:

### Step-by-Step Execution Plan

| Step | Timing | Action | Amount (NIS) | Dilution |

### Why This Strategy Wins

1. **Minimal dilution:** 25-32% total equity given up through seed -- well below the 40%+ that pure VC paths require. IIA grants cover NIS 3.5-5.5M non-dilutively.

2. **Strong validation chain:** Tnufa approval -> angel commitment -> IIA pre-seed -> traction -> VC seed. Each step validates the next, building momentum.

3. **Speed is acceptable:** Tnufa approval takes ~2 months. Angel round runs in parallel. Total time to funded MVP: 5-6 months from start. Not as fast as pure VC, but only 2-3 months slower.

4. **Female founder advantage:** The 10% IIA bonus for female founders adds NIS 150K-500K in non-dilutive capital at each stage. Over the lifecycle, this could total NIS 500K-1M+ in additional free capital.

5. **Risk mitigation:** IIA grants de-risk the early stages. If the MVP fails, founders have lost only 10-12% equity in the angel round (not 20%+ to a VC). Personal financial exposure is limited.

6. **Network through angels:** Carefully selected parent-angels provide domain expertise, user introductions, and emotional investment in the product's success.

### Critical Prerequisites

- **Israeli registered company (Ltd.)** required for IIA grants
- **Female founder status** must be documented for the 10% bonus
- **At least one committed angel investor** before applying for IIA Pre-Seed Fund (IIA requires matching private capital)
- **Technology positioning:** The pitch to IIA must emphasize technological innovation (AI matching, verification systems, safety algorithms) not just "marketplace" or "directory"
