# Emuni Competition Update — March 2026

**Research Date:** March 7, 2026
**Method:** 4 parallel research agents covering Israeli direct, global platforms, indirect/adjacent, and market trends
**Verdict:** The window is wide open. No new entrant. Market tailwinds are stronger than when the PRD was written.

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

After exhaustive research across English and Hebrew queries:

1. **No new Israeli nanny/babysitter app launched in 2025-2026.** Zero.
2. **No global platform has entered or plans to enter Israel.** All major players (Care.com, UrbanSitter, Sittercity, Bubble, Helpr) are US/UK-focused or pivoting to B2B employer benefits.
3. **The real competitor is Facebook groups + WhatsApp** — not any app or platform.
4. **Market tailwinds are stronger:** 114,000 toddlers in unsupervised care, January 2026 daycare tragedy triggering national outcry, foreign caregiver shortage post-war.
5. **Zero VC money entered Israeli childcare in 2025-2026.** No well-funded competitor will appear overnight.

### Key Findings

**Helpbook (helpbook.co.il)** — The most established digital player. Founded 2014 by Yoav Weinshel. Claims to be Israel's leading childcare job board. Has video profiles, employer reviews, recorded interviews. Multi-category (nannies, babysitters, elderly care, cleaning, dog-walking). Available on Google Play (Ionic framework). No iOS app. No booking, no payments, no matching algorithm, no background checks. Monetizes via paid packages for employers to access candidate contact info.

**Babysits Israel** — Global platform (Netherlands, founded 2008). 7M+ users worldwide, 4.4/5 app rating globally. But only **89 active babysitters in Tel Aviv** and 127 families nationwide. Average rate NIS 52-64/hr. Despite years of having babysits.co.il, they have essentially zero traction. English-first interface kills adoption.

**Ozeretli (עוזרתלי)** — Modern Angular-based job board covering babysitters, nannies, special-needs caregivers. International versions (US, UK, Israel). Has a PWA on Google Play. Notable for special-needs caregiver niche. No marketplace features.

**Babysitter.co.il** — 24-year-old directory (since 2002). 3,477 registered caregivers. HTTP-only, no HTTPS. Looks like a 2005-era website. No app, no modern features. Proves demand exists but nothing else.

**Metapele (metapele.co.il)** — Traditional nanny agency. Claims "oldest and most professional" in Israel. Team includes social workers. Wix website. Small, professional, but no tech platform.

**ExitValley "Babysitting" app** — The only prior marketplace attempt. Founded ~2016 by Nir Zilber. Reached 34,000 users in beta. Had in-app payments, location matching, 4% commission. Partnered with MasterCard Israel and AmEx. Planned US/Europe expansion. **Dead since ~2018.** Cause of death: likely underfunding (crowdfunding only), premature international expansion, unsustainable 4% commission on small transactions.

### What No Israeli Competitor Has

Not a single competitor offers ALL of these together:
- Hebrew-first mobile app (iOS + Android)
- Identity verification (teudat zehut)
- Background check badges
- Real-time booking & scheduling
- Integrated payments (Bit/PayMe)
- Reviews & ratings
- Safety features (location sharing, emergency buttons)
- Matching algorithm

**This is Emuni's whitespace.**

## PART 3: INDIRECT & ADJACENT COMPETITORS

### 3.1 Facebook Groups (Threat: MEDIUM-HIGH)

The de facto childcare discovery layer in Israel:

| Group | Focus | Region |
|-------|-------|--------|
| "Parents from TLV looking for caregiver/babysitter" | Direct nanny search | Tel Aviv |
| **"Nannies & caregivers North TLV"** | Caregiver listings | **Old North TLV (Emuni's launch zone)** |
| "Babysitting Services in Tel Aviv area" | English-language | Tel Aviv |
| TLV Parents Community | General parenting + recs | Tel Aviv (~23,500 members) |
| "Parents developing children's thinking" | General parenting | National (~80,300 members) |

**Why they're beatable:** Zero search/filter, no profiles, no reviews, no verification, ephemeral (posts disappear), no accountability, privacy concerns posting publicly, no booking/payment.

**Why they're dangerous:** Free, trusted (social graph = implicit vetting), deeply habitual. Israeli parents' first instinct is to post in a Facebook group.

### 3.2 Yad2 (Threat: LOW — but EXISTENTIAL if they enter)

- Israel's dominant classifieds ($950M-$1.23B valuation)
- Revenue: real estate 56%, autos 27%, jobs 15%
- **Has NOT entered childcare.** No dedicated babysitter/nanny category exists
- Their DNA is list-and-forget classifieds, not trust-intensive marketplace
- Speed to market matters — Yad2 could wake up any quarter

### 3.3 iSavta (Threat: LOW now — MEDIUM-HIGH if they pivot to childcare)

- 35,400+ registered caregivers, 20,000+ employers
- Subscription model, profile-based matching
- Currently **elderly care ONLY**
- Closest structural analog to Emuni in Israel
- Brand ("savta" = grandmother) tied to elderly care — hard to rebrand
- Supply is mostly foreign workers (Filipino) — different from local babysitter market
- **No evidence of childcare pivot plans**

### 3.4 WhatsApp (Threat: LOW-MEDIUM)

- Used for informal neighborhood nanny recommendations
- No organized WhatsApp babysitter service found in Israel
- Invisible, unsearchable, unmonetizable
- Represents the "inner circle" trust level that's hardest to replicate

## PART 4: MARKET TAILWINDS & HEADWINDS

### TAILWINDS (Market Growing)

| Factor | Impact | Strength |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **114,000 toddlers in unsupervised care** | Parents desperate for trustworthy options | Very Strong |
| **January 2026 daycare tragedy** (2 infants died, 53 injured in unlicensed Jerusalem daycare) | Trust in informal care collapsing — platform trust features become differentiator | Very Strong |
| **TFR 2.85** (highest in OECD) | Sustained childcare demand for 10+ years | Strong |
| **GDP growth 4.9% forecast 2026** | Economy recovering | Strong |
| **40% of 0-3 children have no formal care** | Massive addressable market for flexible nanny/babysitter solutions | Strong |
| **TLV babysitter rates NIS 58-64/hr** | NIS 49/mo premium = less than 1 hour of babysitting | Strong |
| **Foreign caregiver shortage** (war-related, Filipino recruitment disrupted) | Pushes families toward local nanny/babysitter solutions | Moderate |
| **Motherhood wage penalty: 30% income drop** in first year | Strong economic incentive for mothers to find reliable childcare | Moderate |
| **Government supervision budget cut 76%** (NIS 190M -> NIS 46M) | State can't fix this with enforcement; market solutions needed | Moderate |
| **Childcare tech not VC-overhyped** | Less competition, no well-funded competitor likely to enter | Moderate |
| **Reserve duty childcare gap** (uniquely Israeli) | Families need emergency babysitting when parent called to reserves | Moderate |

### HEADWINDS (Watch Carefully)

| Factor | Impact | Strength |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Secular fertility declining toward 1.7** | Long-term market contraction in target segment | Weak (10yr+ horizon) |
| **Negative net migration** (unprecedented) | Fewer new families arriving; also fewer caregivers | Moderate |
| **Consumer spending cautious** | Durable goods -10%, but childcare is essential, not discretionary | Weak |
| **Proposed Early Childhood Authority** | Could extend regulation to platforms eventually — but focused on daycares with 7+ children | Weak (years away) |

### Net Assessment

**The market opportunity is STRONGER in March 2026 than when the PRD was written.** The January 2026 daycare tragedy has put childcare safety at the top of the national conversation. The combination of 114K unsupervised toddlers + foreign caregiver shortage + reserve duty gaps creates unprecedented demand for trustworthy, flexible childcare solutions.

## PART 6: STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### 1. Lean Into Trust/Safety Messaging
The daycare tragedy puts "verified profiles + reviews + safety features" at the center of national conversation. This is Emuni's moment. Position as the safe, trustworthy alternative.

### 2. The Battle Is Against Inertia, Not Competitors
No app competitor exists. The real fight is convincing parents to switch from "post in my Facebook group" to using Emuni. The app must be **easier than writing a Facebook post**.

### 3. Defensibility = Trust Infrastructure
If Yad2 or iSavta enters later, they bring traffic but not trust. Emuni's moat is accumulated reviews, verified profiles, background check badges, and safety features. These compound over time and cannot be cloned overnight.

### 4. Bubble (UK) Is the Model to Study
Near-bootstrapped (~GBP 650K seed). ~GBP 10M revenue. 300K+ community. 1-in-4 acceptance rate. Social trust layer. Insurance per sit. This is what Emuni should aspire to.

### 5. B2B Is a Y2-Y3 Play
Every major global platform is pivoting to employer-benefits (Care.com, UrbanSitter, Helpr). Israeli employers need childcare solutions too, especially during reserve duty. Plan for B2B but don't launch with it.

### 6. Learn From the Dead
The ExitValley "Babysitting" app reached 34K users then died. Lessons: don't crowdfund (insufficient capital), don't expand internationally before proving Israel, and 4% commission on NIS 200 transactions is not a business.

### 7. Speed Matters
Yad2 could launch a childcare category any quarter. iSavta could add a childcare vertical. Move fast, accumulate trust data, and build a moat before anyone notices.
