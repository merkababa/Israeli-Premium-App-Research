# Livy & Dayarim -- Deep Competitive Intelligence Report

**Date**: March 2, 2026
**Analyst**: Competitive Intelligence Agent
**Classification**: Internal -- Strategic Planning

---

## Executive Summary

Livy and Dayarim are two smaller Israeli Vaad Bayit management platforms that occupy the lower end of the market. Neither represents a significant competitive threat individually, but together they illustrate the fragmented landscape of micro-competitors we will encounter. **Dayarim is the more established of the two** (operating since 2008, 1,700+ building committees at peak), while **Livy is a one-person operation** with a poor-quality app (1.0-star iOS rating). Both are vulnerable to displacement by a free, modern alternative.

## SECTION 4: COMBINED WIN STRATEGY

### 4.1 Why Neither Is a Real Threat

1. **Neither has modern technology**: Both use dated frameworks (Ionic/Angular, ASP.NET). Neither has WhatsApp integration, which is the dominant communication channel in Israeli buildings.

2. **Neither has meaningful scale**: Combined, they likely serve fewer than 2,000 buildings. The Israeli market has ~350,000+ residential buildings. They have penetrated less than 1% of the addressable market.

3. **Neither has funding or growth trajectory**: Both are bootstrapped micro-businesses with no visible growth strategy, marketing budget, or expansion plans.

4. **Neither is free**: Both charge building committees, creating adoption friction. Our free model eliminates the single biggest barrier to adoption for volunteer committees.

### 4.2 How to Absorb Their Users

**Strategy for Livy Users (estimated <100 buildings):**
- These users are paying NIS 49-69/month for a broken product (1.0 stars)
- **Direct outreach**: If we can identify Livy buildings (Facebook community has 238 members), we can target them with "switch to free" messaging
- **Pain point**: App crashes and reliability issues mean these users are already looking for alternatives
- **Migration tool**: Offer a simple CSV import of resident data from Livy
- **Timeline**: These users should be easy to convert in months 1-3 post-launch

**Strategy for Dayarim Users (estimated 500-1,700 buildings):**
- These users have been with Dayarim for years -- switching cost is emotional (inertia), not financial
- **Lead with WhatsApp**: Dayarim has no WhatsApp integration. Offer residents the ability to receive payment reminders and updates via WhatsApp, which is already how Israeli building committees communicate
- **Lead with iOS**: Dayarim has no iOS app. Any building with iPhone-using committee members or residents is underserved
- **Lead with free**: Even NIS 30/month for a 20-unit building is a cost that our free model eliminates
- **Legal consultation angle**: Dayarim's free legal consultation is a real differentiator -- consider partnering with a legal service or offering template warning letters for non-payers
- **Timeline**: Dayarim users will be slower to convert (months 6-12) due to inertia. Target buildings whose Dayarim subscriptions come up for renewal or whose committees change (new vaad elections happen annually in many buildings)

### 4.3 Defensive Positioning

**Against Livy evolving:**
- Livy is a solo developer with a broken product. The probability of a competitive leap is very low.
- If Livy somehow improves and drops to free, our WhatsApp-native approach and modern tech stack will still differentiate.

**Against Dayarim evolving:**
- Dayarim is 16 years old on ASP.NET. A full modernization would be expensive and risky for a bootstrapped micro-business.
- Dayarim's strength is customer loyalty and legal services -- not technology. If they tried to modernize, they would likely break things for existing users.
- The biggest risk is Dayarim getting acquired by a larger player (like Ding/OXS or Bllink) for its user base. This seems unlikely given the small scale.

### 4.4 Key Takeaways for Our Product

1. **Per-resident pricing works for cheap but not for free**: Dayarim proves that even NIS 1.50/resident creates friction. Our 1.5% transaction fee model avoids this entirely.

2. **Legal services are valued**: Dayarim's free legal consultation is mentioned positively. Consider adding template legal letters or partnering with a legal-tech provider.

3. **Virtual voting is a real feature need**: Both Livy and Dayarim offer some form of surveys/voting. Building assemblies (asefot dayarim) are legally required, and digital voting saves time.

4. **"Good enough" is the real enemy**: Dayarim's biggest asset is that it works well enough for buildings that already use it. We need to offer a 10x better experience, not just 2x, to overcome inertia.

5. **Mobile quality matters enormously**: Livy's 1.0-star rating proves that a bad app is worse than no app. We must ship a high-quality mobile experience from day one.

6. **No iOS = half the market lost**: Dayarim's lack of an iOS app is a critical weakness. We must launch on both platforms simultaneously.
