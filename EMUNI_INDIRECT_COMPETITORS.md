# Emuni -- Indirect & Adjacent Competitor Research

**Date:** March 7, 2026
**Scope:** Facebook groups, Yad2, WhatsApp, domestic worker platforms, home-services apps, Janglo, parent communities, staffing agencies
**Purpose:** Map every channel Israeli parents currently use to find childcare that is NOT a dedicated nanny marketplace app

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Israeli parents today solve the "find a babysitter" problem through a fragmented patchwork of Facebook groups, WhatsApp neighborhood chats, legacy job boards (Helpbook, Babysitter.co.il, Ozeretli), boutique agencies (Nanny Poppins, Rockmybaby, City Sitters, Concierge-Me), and the international platform Babysits. There is no dominant, mobile-first, trust-centric marketplace.

**Key findings:**

1. **Facebook groups are the #1 channel** -- multiple groups with 5K-80K+ members serve as the de facto childcare discovery layer. Zero vetting, zero structure, but deeply trusted because of social proof.
2. **Helpbook.co.il is the largest structured platform** (since 2014, "tens of thousands" of users) but remains a static job board with no app, no real-time booking, and no background checks.
3. **Boutique agencies** (Nanny Poppins, Rockmybaby, City Sitters, Concierge-Me) serve the English-speaking/tourist niche. High-touch, high-price, small scale. None are mobile-first marketplaces.
4. **iSavta is the closest structural analog** -- 35,400+ caregivers, 20,000+ patients, subscription model -- but focused entirely on elderly care. If iSavta pivoted to childcare, it would be a serious competitor.
5. **Yad2 has NOT entered childcare** despite being Israel's #1 classifieds platform ($950M-$1.23B valuation). They remain focused on real estate, autos, and jobs. This is a window of opportunity.
6. **Wolt is becoming an "everything app"** (35% non-food orders) but has NOT signaled any move into people-services or childcare. The regulatory scrutiny it faces makes expansion into employment-adjacent services unlikely near-term.
7. **No TaskRabbit/Handy equivalent exists in Israel** for home services generally. The market is wide open for a vertical marketplace.
8. **WhatsApp communities** are growing as a channel but are invisible, unsearchable, and unmonetizable -- they validate demand but cannot be competed against directly.

### 1.2 How They Work

- **Parents post:** "Looking for babysitter in [neighborhood], [days/hours], [age of children]"
- **Caregivers reply** in comments or DMs with availability and rates
- **Recommendations** happen organically: "I used [name], she was great"
- Some groups have admins who enforce posting rules (e.g., no duplicate posts within X days)
- No vetting, no profiles, no reviews, no booking, no payment

### 1.3 Threat Assessment: **MEDIUM-HIGH**

Facebook groups are Emuni's #1 incumbent competitor -- not because they're good, but because they're free, trusted (social graph = implicit vetting), and deeply habitual. Israeli parents' first instinct is to post in a Facebook group.

**Why they're beatable:**
- Zero search/filter capability (you scroll or you miss it)
- No caregiver profiles, no reviews, no verification
- Ephemeral -- posts disappear into the feed within hours
- No accountability -- bad experiences go unreported
- Privacy concerns -- posting about childcare needs publicly
- No booking, scheduling, or payment infrastructure

**What Emuni can learn:**
- The hamlatza (recommendation) culture is everything. Emuni must replicate the "my friend vouches for her" feeling digitally
- Hyperlocal matters -- groups are organized by neighborhood, not city
- The "North Tel Aviv" nanny group confirms Old North TLV as the right launch zone
- Free is the default expectation -- Emuni's free tier (1 booking/month) correctly addresses this

## 2. YAD2 / MADLAN

### 2.1 Current State

Yad2 is Israel's dominant classifieds platform (acquired by Apax Partners for ~$950M, later reportedly valued at ~$1.23B). Revenue breakdown: real estate 56%, autos 27%, jobs 15%.

**Yad2 does NOT currently have a dedicated childcare/nanny/babysitter category.** Their services vertical does not appear to include childcare matching. They acquired Drushim.il (jobs site) and have expanded B2B data monetization, but have not moved into people-services marketplaces.

Madlan (Yad2's real estate data platform) remains focused exclusively on property data and has not expanded into adjacent home services.

### 2.2 Threat Assessment: **LOW (but existential if they enter)**

- Yad2 has the brand, the traffic, and the trust to launch a childcare category overnight
- However, childcare is a trust-intensive vertical that requires identity verification, background checks, and review systems -- none of which Yad2 has built
- Yad2's DNA is classifieds (list-and-forget), not marketplace (ongoing relationship)
- Their regulatory scrutiny (Competition Authority involvement) may slow expansion
- **Risk window:** If Emuni achieves product-market fit before Yad2 notices the opportunity, the head start in trust infrastructure (verification, reviews, safety features) creates a defensible moat

**What Emuni can learn:**
- Yad2's absence confirms no one has built this properly yet
- Speed to market matters -- Yad2 could wake up any quarter

## 3. WHATSAPP-BASED SERVICES

### 3.1 How It Works

WhatsApp is used for childcare discovery in two ways in Israel:

1. **Neighborhood/building WhatsApp groups:** Parents ask "does anyone know a good babysitter?" in their building or street group. This is the most trusted channel because it's the smallest social circle.
2. **WhatsApp Communities:** Larger organized communities (500+ members) for specific cities or topics. Some include childcare recommendation threads.

There are 533+ Israel WhatsApp group links cataloged on aggregator sites, covering cities, interests, and communities. Some caregiver platforms (like Helpbook) mention WhatsApp as a contact method on individual profiles.

### 3.2 No Organized WhatsApp Babysitter Services Found

Despite thorough searching, no organized WhatsApp-based nanny/babysitter matching service was found operating in Israel. This contrasts with some markets (e.g., India, Brazil) where WhatsApp-native services exist.

### 3.3 Threat Assessment: **LOW-MEDIUM**

- WhatsApp is invisible to search, impossible to scale, and creates zero accountability
- However, it represents the "inner circle" trust level that is extremely hard to replicate
- WhatsApp communities are growing in Israel and could become a more organized channel

**What Emuni can learn:**
- The booking confirmation / chat flow in Emuni should feel as natural as WhatsApp messaging
- Consider WhatsApp sharing: "Share this nanny's profile on WhatsApp" as a growth mechanic
- The trust hierarchy is: (1) my friend told me on WhatsApp > (2) Facebook group post > (3) any platform. Emuni needs to crack level 1.

## 6. INTERNATIONAL PLATFORMS WITH ISRAEL PRESENCE

### 6.1 Babysits (babysits.co.il)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | International babysitting marketplace |
| **Israel presence** | 89 active babysitters in Tel Aviv, 28 in Petah Tikva |
| **Global scale** | "Millions of families and childcare providers worldwide" |
| **Features** | Identity verification, automated profile checks, messaging, booking + payment, reviews, references, transparent pricing |
| **Pricing** | Basic features free; premium subscription for full access |
| **App** | Yes (Android + iOS) |

**Threat Assessment: MEDIUM**

Babysits has the right product architecture (verification, booking, payment, reviews) but minimal Israel traction (~89 sitters in TLV is tiny). Their challenge is localization -- the platform feels generic and non-Israeli. Hebrew support exists but the product is not culturally optimized.

**Why Emuni beats it:**
- Hebrew-first UX vs. translated interface
- Israel-specific trust signals (teudat zehut verification, IDF service as social proof)
- Local payment methods (Bit, PayMe) vs. international payment rails
- Cultural understanding of hamlatza, mishpachton dynamics, Israeli work culture
- 89 sitters in TLV = basically zero supply

**What Emuni can learn:**
- Babysits proves the freemium model works globally for babysitting marketplaces
- Their safety feature set (verification, messaging monitoring, references) is the minimum bar
- Low Israel traction after years of operation confirms a localized player can win

### 6.2 GreatAuPair / AuPair.com

International au pair matching platforms with Israel listings. Not direct competitors -- they serve the au pair niche (live-in, usually foreign workers). Emuni's market is local Israeli babysitters/nannies.

**Threat Assessment: NEGLIGIBLE** for the core Emuni use case.

## 7. DOMESTIC WORKER PLATFORMS (Adjacent)

### 7.1 iSavta

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Online caregiver matching platform |
| **Focus** | Elderly care (NOT childcare) |
| **Registered caregivers** | 35,400+ |
| **Registered patients/employers** | 20,000+ |
| **Pricing** | Subscription plans (one-time payment for 365 days of full access); managed service option |
| **Features** | Profile filtering (location, specialty, gender), salary calculator, agency discounts, direct messaging |
| **Website** | isavta.co.il |

**Threat Assessment: LOW currently, MEDIUM-HIGH if they pivot**

iSavta is the closest structural analog to what Emuni wants to build. They have:
- Large registered user base (55K+ total)
- Two-sided marketplace dynamics
- Subscription monetization
- Profile-based matching

**Why this matters:**
If iSavta decided to add a "childcare" vertical, they already have the platform, the brand recognition, and a portion of the caregiver supply (some elderly caregivers also do childcare). This is the single most credible adjacency threat.

**Mitigating factors:**
- Elderly care and childcare are culturally different markets in Israel
- iSavta's brand = "savta" (grandmother) = elderly care. Hard to rebrand
- Their caregiver supply is heavily foreign workers (Filipino, etc.) -- different from the local Israeli babysitter market
- No evidence of expansion plans

**What Emuni can learn:**
- 35,400 registered caregivers on a niche platform proves Israel has appetite for digital caregiver matching
- Subscription model works in the Israeli caregiver market
- The salary calculator is a smart retention feature Emuni could adapt (nanny rate calculator)

- The absence of a dedicated Israeli parent app is an opportunity. Emuni could become the "parenting trust layer" beyond just babysitting.

## 13. STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR EMUNI

### 13.1 The Window Is Open

No player combines: (1) Hebrew-first mobile app + (2) identity verification + (3) real-time booking + (4) reviews/trust signals + (5) safety features. This is Emuni's whitespace.

### 13.2 The Real Competition Is Inertia

The biggest competitor is not a company -- it is the habit of posting in a Facebook group. Emuni's growth strategy must make the app easier than writing a Facebook post. The "one-tap rebook" and "saved nannies" features are critical for this.

### 13.3 Defensibility Comes From Trust Infrastructure

If Yad2 or iSavta enters childcare, they will bring traffic but not trust. Emuni's moat is:
- Verified caregiver profiles with background check badges
- Accumulated reviews and ratings
- Safety features (location sharing, emergency buttons)
- Community trust built through successful bookings

These compound over time and cannot be cloned overnight.

### 13.4 The English-Speaking Niche Is Already Served (Poorly)

Nanny Poppins, Rockmybaby, City Sitters, Concierge-Me, and Janglo collectively serve the English-speaking expat/tourist market. This is a small, fragmented niche. Emuni's decision to launch Hebrew-first is validated -- the mass market is unserved.

### 13.5 Pricing Benchmarks

- **Rockmybaby:** NIS 50 booking fee + NIS 40/hour (agency-mediated)
- **Helpbook:** Paid packages for employers (amount unknown)
- **iSavta:** Annual subscription for full access
- **Babysits:** Freemium (basic free, premium subscription)
- **Emuni planned:** Free (1 booking/mo) + Premium NIS 49/mo + Per-contact NIS 19

Emuni's pricing is competitive and sits between the free (Facebook/WhatsApp) and expensive (agency NIS 50+ per booking) tiers.
