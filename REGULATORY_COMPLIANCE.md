# Israeli Regulatory Compliance (2026 Focus)

When operating an app in the Israeli market, especially one processing payments and user data, adherence to local regulations is critical. This project specifically avoids highly regulated sectors like fintech or health tech to minimize licensing requirements for the MVP.

## 1. Data Privacy (Privacy Protection Law - Amendment 13)
- **Strict Compliance Required:** Amendment 13 (effective Aug 2025) significantly expanded privacy requirements.
- **Consent:** Must have explicit, clear opt-in consent for data processing.
- **AI Usage:** If AI is used to process user requests or data, detailed disclosures are mandatory, and a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) may be required.
- **Actionable:** Ensure a robust, Hebrew-compliant Privacy Policy and cookie/tracking consent banners.

## 2. Consumer Protection (Consumer Protection Law, 5741-1981)
- **Cancellation Rights:** Israeli law mandates a 14-day cancellation policy for distance sales.
- **Extended Rights:** Special extended cancellation periods (up to 4 months) apply to protected groups (new immigrants, senior citizens, persons with disabilities).
- **Actionable:** The app's refund policy must be legally compliant, clearly stated in Hebrew, and ideally automated to match the ease of booking. Transparent pricing in NIS is mandatory.

## 3. Taxation & Invoicing
- **VAT:** Standard VAT rate is 18% (as of Jan 2025). The platform must account for this in pricing and provider payouts.
- **E-Invoicing Mandate:** By April 1, 2026, Israel requires full digital reporting of invoices via direct API connections to the Tax Authority systems.
- **Actionable:** If operating as a foreign entity, VAT registration via a local representative is needed. For the MVP, ensure the payment/invoicing system can handle or integrate with local e-invoicing requirements by 2026.

## 4. App Store Regulations
- No specific, localized app store regulations (like the EU's DMA) currently exist for Israel. Standard Apple App Store and Google Play Store requirements apply. No local legal entity is mandated solely for distribution.
